# A chronicle of my lost OneNote diaries (and why the Perspective project is a thing)

In January I sought to find my long forgotten 2016 diaries written on
OneNote. And I've exhausted the vast majority, if not ail of my hopes. My
attempts of outreach primarily took place on the (often patronizing) Reddit
and Microsoft communities, though this document is intended to be more than
that.

I want to let anyone who encounters more or less the same situation witness
every step I did over the past months to find my notes. I want to bring
light to the unspeakable horror Microsoft had inflicted on me. I want this
to be a critique of Microsoft’s questionable philosophy of user data, the
software design choices they made, and their clueless if not horrible
support. And last but not least, I want to talk about how I wish things were
handled differently - my lost diaries are a reflection of both my ignorance
of my own digital possessions and the fundamental failure of big
corporations Microsoft to respect user data.

I will be making a blog post about exporting stuff from Apple too. There are
a lot of blog posts to come, actually. In fact this would be one of those if
it were not for the depressing nature of the outcome…

## January 8, 2024

Let me start with the day where it all began. I was just sitting idly in my
physics class, where the teacher left us all to study on our own. Pretty
standard stuff there, and because I know my teacher I took out my laptop
(like usual) and plugged in my Seagate drive (which was anything but usual:
I only remembered the drive when I went to my old apartment to tidy things
up that weekend and found it under some entangled wires in a metal tray.)
This was the first time I plugged it in over nearly 6 years, so I knew I was
in for some throwback.

My guess was correct. I could not believe what I found in that hard drive:
some I knew existed but didn't know where to begin to find, like some
Minecraft screen recordings in 2016-ish and a Clash Royale video I recorded
and edited in 2017. Some forgotten, like the really old videos I filmed on
my phone between 2016 and 2017 - videos and screenshots where I was still
a primary school student, where my little brother was a chubby toddler who
just wouldn't budge at the weird noises I made (trust me you know it was
weird when even I think it's weird), where my classmates would be chatting
in QQ (crazy!!! I know QQ isn't completely dead but Weixin had since taken
over as the de facto instant messaging + social media app…)

Among all the videos and screenshots one thing stood out… one thing that
shined like gold that day - a screenshot of one sole diary entry, written
fully in Chinese, about my fifth grade sports day.

I was stunned that I had forgotten about such an important thing. While it
is true that I had been reading and writing diaries in Perspective ever
since 2018, I was at least aware that my Daylio diaries exist and predate
them. I was totally unaware that there are digital diaries with significant
effort that come before even the Daylio diaries - not to mention 2016?!
That's when I just got the phone from my dad! I never knew I began writing
diaries THAT early - and you read the title and it says “day 23” even… that
means there are possibly 22 other entries just like this!

I was over-the-roof kind of excited that day - scrap pleasing school and
getting good grades! I'm gonna get my 2016 diaries-

Oh wait, how….

See there is just one issue: I have no idea where these diaries are. I can
see it’s OneNote but I can't tell which account (if it was any account I use
now I would've seen the blatantly obviously important one-of-a-kind notebook
far far back in time).

That day I immediately checked my old Microsoft accounts, endfindme and
2462257029 (both ending with outlook dot com FYI). Good thing I had been
going through my old password credentials since October 2023 (thanks to June
me recovering my 10-year-old Google account and September (-ish?) me
unlocking my password manager there), so logging in was no rocket science.

…except just as I had remembered when I reset the passwords and logged in in
October, both accounts had a completely clean OneDrive. (For those of you
wondering what OneDrive has to do with all this, OneNote is stored on
OneDrive. So empty OneDrive means bye-bye OneNote.)

I checked both accounts again and again, looking harder each time - empty.
Opening the web version of OneNote even helped me create a new notebook.

I was heartbroken. I almost vomited out of sadness. The same day
I remembered these diaries and had my whole day brightened by the survival
of one of them had to also be the day I mourn the irrevocable loss of the
rest of those diaries - those twenty-or-so entires that could have survived.

…or was that it?

## A brief timeline of my diaries

You may have wondered why I didn't start with searching in the phone and
instead started with OneNote. Well, to explain that there's a bit of
something you must know then… the arduous implication of getting these
diaries from the phone.

As I said the believed start of my diaries was Perspective, the iPhone app
which the Perspective backup project was named after. I had been writing
stuff in it since 2018 but the app’s development seemed to have halted for
a very long time. I was a loyal user, and I’d been patiently waiting for an
export function to arrive any day now, any day now… until I gave up waiting
and went “screw it I'll do it myself” - that was 2021, what gave birth to of
basicproc.py and the psp library, the code which I personally used to
organize and display my diaries outside of the app. (It might sound like
I keep emphasizing this point, but please bear with me - it's important and
it's for YOUR own sake, dear person reading this document.)

As for the difficulty accessing the Perspective diaries? Well, not at all
difficult so far. In fact as I’m drafting this on my phone I opened the
Perspective app on my home screen: all my diaries since 2018 are there,
offline, ready to be read and even edited to this day - but that's only
because I am using the same phone ever since that time - well, same brand at
least. I upgraded my iPhone due to accidentally breaking the screen of the
previous one, but because migrating data from one iPhone to a new one is
virtually seamless every day just rolled on fine. The story would have been
completely different if I had switched to a non-iPhone phone. Luckily 2021
me was smart enough to realize this and promptly backed years worth of
diaries in many sittings.

Things were much less convenient for my Daylio diaries, next to infuriating
for Journey and Pendo, and catastrophic for apps like me.time (which doesn't
even exist on the Google Play store anymore! seriously!) I can only access
them through an Android emulator, then log in to Google Play (somehow - some
emulators can't even do that), then download Daylio and choose to restore
whatever backup that happened to store a full copy of my diaries.

And even then the diaries only stay there - the moment I close the emulator
I am effectively locked out of my diaries. Now, I say Daylio is the mildest
offender here because it at least lets you export - for one because 2017 me
had the enthusiasm for Daylio to buy a Japanese gift card and use the
redeemed credit to unlock premium features I can choose to export all my
diaries as a neatly rendered PDF. (Did that once in 2019 on MeMu or
something? The emulator could run Clash Royale but the emojis were all
utterly butchered DX re-exported in 2024 with Android Emulator (which
I recommend!) and the fonts are all rescued! except they're the boring round
emojis and no blob emojis… you know how I feel 😡) And for two - and this
seems like a new thing the Daylio team added by the way - is that you can
export a FULL backup which you can carry around with you and potentially
RESTORE at a later time. (Even better Daylio lets you do that regardless of
whether you pay them the premium plan! W for the whole Daylio team!!! XD)

Okay maybe the latter part is usually too wonderful of a thing to ask for…
but the first part is really the cream of the crop - and I'll teach you how
the make the most of it with Daylio in this case!

**Step 1**: export your entries as a file. you can do that by navigating to
More > Backup & Restore > Advanced Options > Export. if that isn't super
clear i made a video of it here (link here soon). note: do not use to Export
Entries > CSV, as that gives you an export with region-specific dates and
gobbled newline characters (if you ever write entries on multiple lines
which I always do). (you are always welcome to keep a copy of it though!
CSV is a more friendly format which you can import with relative ease with
a spreadsheet app! ;)

**Step 2**: move that file to a computer you're comfortable using.
i'll use my MacBook to demonstrate.
